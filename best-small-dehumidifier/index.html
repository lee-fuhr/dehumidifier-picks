Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between a thermoelectric and a compressor dehumidifier?

Thermoelectric (Peltier) dehumidifiers use a solid-state cooling element to condense moisture. They are very quiet and compact but remove significantly less moisture per day than compressor units. They suit small enclosed spaces with mild humidity. Compressor dehumidifiers work like a refrigerator and remove moisture at much higher volumes. For any real humidity problem beyond a small enclosed space, compressor units are the right tool.

Are small dehumidifiers worth it for a bathroom?

A small thermoelectric unit placed in a bathroom can reduce lingering humidity after showers and prevent the musty smell that builds up in poorly ventilated bathrooms. It is not a substitute for a working exhaust fan, but as a supplementary measure in a small space it provides real benefit. Keep the tank emptied regularly since bathroom air tends to be consistently humid.
